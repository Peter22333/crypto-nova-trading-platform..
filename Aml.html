Anti-Money Laundering Policy

Last updated: January 1, 2025 · Crypto Nova maintains strict AML/KYC procedures in compliance with global financial regulations.

1. Our Commitment

Crypto Nova Trading Limited is committed to the highest standards of anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorist financing (CTF) compliance. We implement robust Know Your Customer (KYC) procedures to ensure our platform is not used for financial crime.

Our AML programme is designed to comply with the EU's 6th Anti-Money Laundering Directive (6AMLD), FATF recommendations, and all applicable national regulations in the jurisdictions where we operate.

2. Know Your Customer (KYC)

All users must complete identity verification before accessing full platform functionality. Our KYC process includes:

  • Identity Verification: Government-issued photo ID (passport, national identity card, or driver's licence)
  • Proof of Address: Utility bill or bank statement dated within the last 3 months
  • Enhanced Due Diligence: Additional documentation may be required for high-value accounts or elevated risk profiles
  • Ongoing Monitoring: Periodic reverification may be required to maintain account access

3. Transaction Monitoring

We continuously monitor all transactions for suspicious activity using automated systems and manual review. Indicators that may trigger enhanced review include:

  • Transactions inconsistent with stated investment objectives or financial profile
  • Rapid layering — frequent deposits followed immediately by withdrawals
  • Transactions involving high-risk jurisdictions or sanctioned addresses
  • Structuring — breaking large transactions into smaller amounts to avoid reporting thresholds
  • Use of privacy coins or mixing services without legitimate business justification

4. Sanctions Screening

All users and transactions are screened against international sanctions lists including OFAC (US), EU Consolidated Sanctions List, UN Security Council, and HM Treasury (UK) lists. Accounts matching sanctioned persons or entities will be immediately frozen pending investigation.

5. Suspicious Activity Reporting

Crypto Nova has appointed a dedicated Money Laundering Reporting Officer (MLRO). Where we have reasonable grounds to suspect money laundering or terrorist financing, we are legally required to file a Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) with the relevant Financial Intelligence Unit. We are prohibited by law from "tipping off" users when a SAR has been filed.

6. Record Keeping

In accordance with regulatory requirements, we retain all customer identification documents and transaction records for a minimum of 5 years after account closure, and 7 years for transaction records. All records are stored securely and are available to competent authorities upon lawful request.

7. Staff Training

All Crypto Nova staff with customer-facing or financial roles receive mandatory AML/CTF training upon joining and annually thereafter. Our compliance team receives enhanced training and stays current with regulatory developments.

8. Prohibited Uses

The following activities are strictly prohibited on the Crypto Nova platform:

  • Deposits or withdrawals connected to proceeds of crime
  • Financing of terrorism or proliferation of weapons of mass destruction
  • Transactions on behalf of sanctioned individuals or entities
  • Use of the platform to conceal the true source or ownership of funds
  • Any activity that violates applicable AML/CTF laws

9. Consequences of Violations

Users found to be in breach of this policy will have their accounts immediately suspended and funds frozen pending investigation. We will cooperate fully with law enforcement authorities and may refer matters for criminal prosecution.

10. Contact Compliance

For any compliance-related queries, please contact our compliance team at compliance@cryptonova.com. To report suspicious activity you have witnessed on the platform, use the same address.
